Effiel Tower

hen the tower opened in the 19th century, it was the world's tallest building at 1,024 feet (312.11 meters). The Eiffel Tower has 1,665 stairs and three viewing platforms. Nearly 50 miles of electric cables cover the structure. There are 120 antennas atop the Eiffel Tower.

Taj Mahal

Taj Mahal is essentially a tomb built in the memory of Shah Jahan's wife, Mumtaz Mahal. Taj Mahal is also famous as the symbol of love. The popular dome and the pillars around the Taj Mahal are common in medieval Mughal architecture. In 1982, the Taj Mahal was designated as a World Heritage Site by UNESCO.

Qutub Minar

Qutub Minar is the second tallest and ever attractive historical monument of India located in Delhi at Aurobindo Marg, Mehrauli. It is built in unique architectural style using red sandstones and marble. It is considered that Mughals built this victory tower to celebrate their victories over the Rajputs.
